In CSS, there are several units you can use to specify font sizes. Here are the most common ones:

Absolute units:

px (pixels)
pt (points)
pc (picas)
in (inches)
cm (centimeters)
mm (millimeters)
Relative units:

em (relative to the font-size of the element)
rem (relative to the font-size of the root element)
% (percentage, relative to the parent element's font size)
vw (viewport width)
vh (viewport height)
vmin (minimum of viewport width and height)
vmin (minimum of viewport width and height)
vmax (maximum of viewport width and height)
ex (relative to the x-height of the current font)
ch (relative to the width of the "0" (zero) character)
